# System Design Assistant — VISION

## North Star
> Be every engineer's **AI co-architect** — turning design from a whiteboard art into a grounded, persistent, reviewable discipline.

## Picture
System design today is ad-hoc: whiteboards, tribal knowledge, stale diagrams, unrecorded decisions. The vision: an AI that designs *with* you — proposing options, reasoning about tradeoffs, drawing diagrams-as-code, drafting ADRs, and (crucially) grounding it all in your *actual* system (via #2) rather than generic patterns. Designs become living, versioned artifacts.

## Two markets (intentional)
1. **Enterprise architecture:** teams design + document + review systems; ADRs + diagrams + tradeoff memory. Sold as a ContextOS module.
2. **Interview prep (prosumer):** practice system-design interviews with an AI interviewer/coach. Proven paid niche; standalone funnel + revenue.

## Phases
| Horizon | What |
|---------|------|
| Y1 | Conversational design + diagrams + ADRs + interview-prep |
| Y2 | Codebase-grounded design (#2) + design memory + team review |
| Y3 | Enterprise architecture governance/standards; ContextOS module |

## Principles
1. **Grounded in real code** (not generic advice) — the differentiator.
2. Diagram-as-code + ADRs (versionable, diffable, durable).
3. Persistent design memory (decisions don't evaporate).
4. Two clear markets; don't blur them.
5. Reuse #2 for grounding, `packages/evals` for quality.

## The bet
That AI co-architecture is a real niche (esp. when grounded in your code), that interview-prep is a reliable adjacent revenue stream, and that this is most powerful as a ContextOS module + a focused prosumer product — not a standalone giant.
